# Quantum Computing and the Threat to Cryptographic Systems

A comprehensive cybersecurity research project led and authored by Aanal Kapadiya exploring the impact of quantum computing on modern cryptographic systems and digital security infrastructure. This research analyzes how emerging quantum technologies threaten traditional encryption methods while evaluating the future of post-quantum cryptography (PQC), quantum resistant encryption standards, and next generation cybersecurity solutions. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

## Overview

Quantum computing represents one of the most disruptive technological advancements in modern computing. While offering groundbreaking opportunities in optimization, artificial intelligence, and scientific simulation, quantum systems also introduce severe risks to current cryptographic standards used to secure global digital infrastructure.

This paper investigates the vulnerabilities of widely adopted encryption methods including RSA, Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC), AES, and SHA-2 against quantum algorithms such as Shor’s Algorithm and Grover’s Algorithm. The research further explores mitigation strategies including Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC), Quantum Key Distribution (QKD), and lattice-based cryptographic systems. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Technical Topics Covered

### Shor’s Algorithm
Examines how quantum factorization algorithms threaten the security of RSA and ECC by efficiently solving integer factorization and discrete logarithm problems previously considered computationally infeasible. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

### Grover’s Algorithm
Explores quantum search acceleration and its implications for symmetric cryptography, including reduced brute-force complexity against AES encryption standards. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

### Post-Quantum Cryptography
Investigates quantum resistant encryption systems designed to remain secure against both classical and quantum computational attacks.

### Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)
Analyzes secure quantum communication methods leveraging quantum mechanics principles such as superposition and entanglement to detect interception attempts.

## Key Findings

- Quantum computing poses a direct threat to current public-key cryptography systems
- RSA and ECC are highly vulnerable to Shor’s Algorithm
- Symmetric encryption systems are weakened by Grover’s Algorithm
- Post-Quantum Cryptography is critical for future cybersecurity resilience
- NIST standardization efforts are essential for global cryptographic transition
- Organizations must begin preparing for quantum safe infrastructure immediately
