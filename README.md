# Quantum Computing and the Threat to Cryptographic Systems

A comprehensive cybersecurity research project led and authored by Aanal Kapadiya exploring the impact of quantum computing on modern cryptographic systems and digital security infrastructure. This research analyzes how emerging quantum technologies threaten traditional encryption methods while evaluating the future of post-quantum cryptography (PQC), quantum resistant encryption standards, and next generation cybersecurity solutions.

## Overview

Quantum computing represents one of the most disruptive technological advancements in modern computing. While offering groundbreaking opportunities in optimization, artificial intelligence, and scientific simulation, quantum systems also introduce severe risks to current cryptographic standards used to secure global digital infrastructure.

This paper investigates the vulnerabilities of widely adopted encryption methods including RSA, Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC), AES, and SHA-2 against quantum algorithms such as Shor’s Algorithm and Grover’s Algorithm. The research further explores mitigation strategies including Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC), Quantum Key Distribution (QKD), and lattice-based cryptographic systems.

## Key Findings

- Quantum computing poses a direct threat to current public-key cryptography systems
- RSA and ECC are highly vulnerable to Shor’s Algorithm
- Symmetric encryption systems are weakened by Grover’s Algorithm
- Post-Quantum Cryptography is essential for future cybersecurity resilience
- NIST standardization efforts are critical for global cryptographic transition
- Organizations must begin preparing for quantum safe infrastructure immediately
